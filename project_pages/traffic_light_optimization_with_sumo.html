Our project focuses on enhancing ambulance response times in urban areas, a critical factor in ensuring timely medical care. Using Simulation of Urban Mobility (SUMO) and Traffic Control Interface (TraCI), we developed a traffic simulation model. This model accurately depicts the movement of ambulances through complex urban traffic scenarios, considering factors like road networks, traffic signals, and ambulance characteristics. By analyzing data on ambulance movement, including speed, distance traveled, and time, we aim to provide valuable insights to urban planners and emergency services. These insights can inform the optimization of ambulance routes and strategies, potentially leading to improved patient outcomes during emergencies.

  • Developed simulation using SUMO to optimize ambulance response times by analyzing factors such as traffic congestion, traffic lights, and regional traffic rules.
  • Achieved a reduction in average response time by 8%.
  • Implemented GPS-based traffic light optimization for efficient navigation within a reasonable timeframe.
  • Addressed a critical issue in emergency services to improve public safety and save lives.
  • Completed the project within 8 months, demonstrating proficiency in data analysis and simulation techniques.
